Part 1 — Manual Analysis

What the Numbers Show

Before involving AI, the data was analysed using standard Python methods — the same approach any analyst would use. This establishes the baseline that the AI layer is then compared against.

Rating Distribution · Negative Rate by Category · Bottom 5 Products

pandas + matplotlib
What this shows: 217 one-star reviews out of 1,258 total. Clothing leads with 29% negative rate. USB-C Hub is the worst individual product at 2.52 average rating. The numbers tell us what and where — but not why customers are unhappy or what specifically is failing.
Part 2 — AI Layer

What the AI Found

The Claude API was given batches of negative reviews per category and asked to identify recurring complaints, what customers praise, and one specific business recommendation. Here's what it produced.

🤖

AI-Generated Root Cause Analysis — 5 Categories

Clothing — 29% negative
Systematic size grading error
Multiple products running 2+ sizes small — Sports Leggings, Winter Jacket, Running Shoes. AI flagged this as a QC issue in size grading, not random variation. Recommendation: halt production and audit supplier manufacturing standards.
Home & Kitchen — 26.4% negative
Blender: potential recall risk
Chemical smell and disproportionate failure rate flagged as potential off-gassing issue. AI recommended engineering review and temporary delisting — a finding the average rating alone would never surface.
Sports — 25.3% negative
Products breaking within 1–2 uses
Water Bottles, Resistance Bands and Jump Ropes failing within days of purchase. Zero positive feedback in the review set — flagged as a red flag requiring immediate supplier audit and durability testing.
Beauty — 22.7% negative
Artificial fragrance causing physical reactions
Eye Cream, Hair Mask and Face Moisturiser triggering headaches across multiple customers. AI identified this as a formulation quality issue affecting multiple SKUs — a cross-product pattern the numbers couldn't reveal.
Electronics — 22.5% negative
USB-C Hub: connectivity, overheating, structural failure
6 specific complaint types identified — disconnects constantly, overheats after 30 minutes, structural collapse on first use. AI recommended pausing new inventory orders until QA testing confirms compliance. The 2.52 average rating was confirmed as a safety issue, not just a quality issue.

💡 My Insights from This Project

What I Took Away from This Analysis

1

Why this project: "Every analytics team I've spoken to is trying to figure out how to use AI without losing analytical rigour. I built this project to answer that question for myself — not just use AI, but test it against manual analysis and be honest about what it adds and what it doesn't."

2

The technical approach: "I used Python and pandas for the manual analysis first — rating distributions, negative rates by category, worst-performing products. Then I called the Claude API with batches of negative reviews per category and asked it to identify recurring complaints and root causes."

3

The most interesting finding: "The AI flagged the Blender for a potential off-gassing issue based on customers describing a chemical smell. That's a safety concern — not just a quality complaint. The average rating of 2.72 would never tell you that. You need to read the text."

4

Where AI fell short: "It only saw the negative reviews, so it couldn't tell me whether the USB-C Hub's 6 complaints represented 14% or 90% of its reviews. It also made recommendations without knowing anything about the business — supplier contracts, budget, lead times. A human still has to own the decision."

5

The conclusion: "AI is an analyst accelerator, not an analyst replacement. It's best at the part of the job that takes the most time and the least judgment — reading and synthesising large amounts of unstructured text. The judgment about what to do with those findings still belongs to the analyst."
