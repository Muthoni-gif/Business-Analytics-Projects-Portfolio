Key Findings

Three Things the Data Revealed

The £180K overrun isn't random noise spread evenly across departments. It concentrates in specific patterns — two of which are structural problems, not one-off events.

£151K
Marketing Spend: Structural Mis-Budget
Marketing campaign spend exceeded its £50K/month budget every single month of FY2024. This isn't overspending — the budget was simply never updated to reflect actual demand. It accounts for 84% of the total company overrun.
+29%
IT Equipment: An Outdated Baseline
IT Equipment was budgeted at a flat £20K/month all year, but actual spend averaged £27K — exceeded every single month. The budget hasn't kept pace with hardware costs. The fix is re-baselining, not cost-cutting.
−£132K
Training: Underspend Is Not a Saving
Every department underspent Training in every month. It partially offsets the Marketing overrun on paper — but it signals cancelled or deferred programmes across the entire company. That's a long-term productivity risk.
Excel Techniques Used

Key Formulas

The four formulas that power the dynamic drill-down tool.

department_totals.xlsx SUMIF 
-- Total budget for selected department
=SUMIF(
  tblBudget[Department],
  B1,
  tblBudget[Budget]
)

-- Same pattern for Actual and Variance
=SUMIF(tblBudget[Department],B1,tblBudget[Actual])
=SUMIF(tblBudget[Department],B1,tblBudget[Variance])
category_drilldown.xlsx FILTER + UNIQUE 
-- Spill unique categories for chosen dept
=UNIQUE(FILTER(
  tblBudget[Category],
  tblBudget[Department]=B1
))

-- Budget per category (A8# = spill range)
=SUMIFS(
  tblBudget[Budget],
  tblBudget[Department], B1,
  tblBudget[Category], A8#
)
💬 How to Present This in an Interview

The Story Behind the Numbers

1

The business question: "Veridian came in £180K over budget. My job wasn't just to calculate that number — it was to explain where it came from, whether it was avoidable, and what to do about it."

2

The Excel approach: "I loaded the raw CSV through Power Query, named it tblBudget, then built two PivotTables — one by department, one by category. I used SUMIF and FILTER with dynamic arrays to create a drill-down tool where you just type a department name and every number updates automatically."

3

The key finding: "84% of the overrun came from one line — Marketing campaign spend, which exceeded its £50K monthly budget every single month. That's not a spending problem; the budget was wrong. The spend was actually appropriate for what the business needed."

4

The counterintuitive finding: "Training underspent by £132K — which looks like good news. It isn't. It means training programmes were cancelled or deferred across every department. I flagged it separately because a Finance team treating it as a saving would be making a mistake."

5

My recommendations: "Three actions: raise the Marketing Spend budget by 20% for FY2025, re-baseline IT Equipment from £20K to £27K per month, and require a written sign-off for any Training underspend above 15% so it becomes a visible decision, not a quiet deferral."
