Key Code

Core Python Techniques

The three most important code blocks that power this analysis.

rfm_calculation.pypandas
# Calculate RFM metrics per customer
reference_date = df['InvoiceDate'].max() + pd.Timedelta(days=1)

rfm = df.groupby('Customer ID').agg(
    Recency   = ('InvoiceDate', lambda x: (reference_date - x.max()).days),
    Frequency = ('Invoice', 'nunique'),
    Monetary  = ('Revenue', 'sum')
).reset_index()
kmeans_clustering.pyscikit-learn
# Scale data and apply K-Means clustering
scaler = StandardScaler()
rfm_scaled = scaler.fit_transform(rfm[['Recency', 'Frequency', 'Monetary']])

kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=4, random_state=42, n_init=10)
rfm['Cluster'] = kmeans.fit_predict(rfm_scaled)

segment_map = {3: 'Champions', 2: 'Loyal Customers',
               1: 'Regular Customers', 0: 'At Risk'}
rfm['Segment'] = rfm['Cluster'].map(segment_map)
visualization.pymatplotlib
# 4-panel visualization dashboard
fig, axes = plt.subplots(2, 2, figsize=(14, 10))
fig.suptitle('RFM Customer Segmentation Analysis', fontsize=16)

# Scatter plot with cluster colors
scatter_colors = rfm['Cluster'].map({3:'#2ecc71', 2:'#3498db',
                                      1:'#e74c3c', 0:'#f39c12'})
axes[1,0].scatter(rfm['Recency'], rfm['Frequency'],
                  c=scatter_colors, alpha=0.6, s=20)
💡 My Insights From This Project

What I Learned & What the Data Told Me

1

Key finding: Just 4 customers — 0.07% of the entire base — average £436K each in annual revenue. The extreme concentration of value in so few customers was striking. Losing even one Champion would have a disproportionate impact on total revenue.

2

What surprised me: The 1,998 At-Risk customers haven't purchased in 463 days on average — over a year. Yet they still account for £1.5M in historical revenue. A targeted win-back campaign for even 10% of this group could recover £152K in revenue.

3

Business implication: The Regular Customers segment (3,841 customers, £11.5M total revenue) is where the biggest lever lies. It's the largest segment by count — even a modest 10% average spend increase per customer would add over £1.1M to total revenue.

4

What I would do next: Automate this as a monthly scoring pipeline that recalculates RFM scores, tracks segment movements (e.g. Loyal → At Risk), and triggers personalised CRM campaigns tailored to each segment's behaviour profile.
